4. Legal Basis for Non-Refundable Policy

Our non-refundable policy is based on established international legal frameworks that recognize the unique nature of digital content. The standard consumer "cooling-off period" or right of withdrawal is typically waived for digital products where performance (i.e., download or access) begins immediately.

Specifically, our policy aligns with provisions such as Article 16(m) of the EU Consumer Rights Directive (2011/83/EU). This directive exempts "the supply of digital content which is not supplied on a tangible medium if the performance has begun with the consumer's prior express consent and his acknowledgment that he thereby loses his right of withdrawal."

Similarly, consumer protection laws in other jurisdictions, including the United States (such as the California Consumer Privacy Act - CCPA), generally do not mandate refunds for non-returnable, irrevocable digital goods that have been delivered as described.
